Pear Cake "Good Luise"

A delicious and fresh dessert for any gathering, big or small. I find adding a bit of cinnamon sugar to the crust brings the whole dish together.

INGREDIENTS

For the dough:

  • 250 g flour
  • 125 g margarine
  • 65 g sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • (optional) cinnamon

For the filling:

  • 1.5 to 2 kg boiled pear halves (canned)
  • 250 g cream quark
  • 150 g sugar
  • 1 lemon, juice and zest
  • 1/2 whipping cream container
  • 12 g gelatin
  • 2 tbsp pear juice
  • 1 tablet chocolate-fat glaze, or some cocoa

STEPS

  1. After combining the dough ingredients, prepare a short crust and allow 30 minutes to cool, then roll into a rectangle (about 25cm x 35cm) and place on a greased baking sheet. Prick several times with a fork and bake in a preheated oven at 220°C for about 20 minutes.
  2. Drain the pears, collect and set aside the juice. (You can use fresh pears, but they must be shortly steamed previously.) Arrange the pear halves in the cake platter.
  3. Stir the quark, sugar, lemon zest, and juice until frothy. Fold in the whipped cream.
  4. Soak the gelatin in a little pear juice to dissolve and likewise fold it in.
  5. Let the quark-cream mixture spread and solidify on the pears.
  6. Use a water bath to dissolve the chocolate-fat glaze. Baste the cake with it and let it harden again.
